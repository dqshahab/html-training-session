Aerodynamics

Definition

Aerodynamics is the study of how gases, specifically air, interact with objects as they move through it.
It focuses on understanding and analyzing the forces and motions that occur during the flow of air over and around solid objects, such as aircraft, cars, buildings, and even natural structures.
Aerodynamics plays a crucial role in various fields, including aerospace engineering, automotive design, wind energy, and sports.
By studying aerodynamics, engineers and scientists aim to optimize the performance, efficiency, stability, and safety of objects in motion, particularly when they are subjected to air resistance or lift forces.
